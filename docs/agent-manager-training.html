What is an AI Agent Manager?

Before we dive into the Flywheel, let's talk about what you'll actually be doing day-to-day.

Your Mission

You're responsible for making sure our AI agents provide accurate, helpful, and on-brand support to customers. Think of yourself as the coach who transforms raw client knowledge into a high-quality, AI-ready knowledge base — and then continuously improves it based on real performance data.

Your Four Key Areas

📚 Context Engineering

Build and maintain the AI Agent's knowledge base using atomic markdown. Create clear guidance on tone, escalation rules, and definitions.

  • Knowledge Base Construction
  • Guidance & Glossary Creation
  • Client Discovery & Gap Analysis

🧪 Testing & Deployment

Create benchmarks from real customer queries, run staging tests, validate in production, and deploy with approval.

  • Benchmark Creation
  • Staging & Production Testing
  • Client Approval & Deployment

📊 Continuous Improvement

Monitor KPIs, identify patterns and gaps, update knowledge, test changes, and deploy improvements.

  • Performance Monitoring (CSAT, Resolution %)
  • The Optimization Flywheel
  • Stakeholder Feedback Integration

🤝 Collaboration

Work with SDMs, Team Leaders, and Client POCs to ensure AI integrates smoothly with human operations.

  • Cross-Team Integration
  • Operational Reporting
  • Status Updates & Action Plans

What is the Flywheel?

Your daily system for managing AI Agents. Four stages that repeat continuously, making your AI Agent better each cycle.

Train

📚 What AI Agent Knows & How It Behaves

Create Content (facts and information) and Guidance (rules and tone) to teach your AI Agent.

Test

🧪 Verify Before Going Live

Run simulated customer conversations to check if AI Agent answers correctly. Fix problems before real customers see them.

Deploy

🚀 Publish to Live Channels

Set AI Agent live across every channel — chat, email, and phone — for consistent support wherever customers reach out.

Analyze

📊 Learn and Improve

Use AI-powered insights to understand performance, find gaps, and know what to improve in the next training cycle.

Metric What It Measures Why It Matters Target Stage
Faithfulness Does AI Agent use only the facts we trained it on? Low scores = AI Agent making things up. Leads to customer complaints and brand damage. ≥ 0.9 Test
Semantic Similarity Does AI Agent's meaning match our reference answer? Ensures AI Agent gives correct meaning, not just similar-sounding words. ≥ 0.7 Test
Resolution Rate How many questions did AI Agent resolve alone? Higher = fewer human agents needed = lower operational cost. Higher = Better Analyze
Handover % How many questions went to humans? High handover defeats the purpose of AI Agent automation. Lower = Better Analyze
CSAT Are customers happy with AI Agent answers? Direct measure of customer experience. The ultimate success metric. Higher = Better Analyze

Stage 1

📚 Train: Teach Your AI Agent

Training has two parts: Content (what AI Agent knows) and Guidance (how AI Agent behaves).

📖 Content — What AI Agent Knows

+

Content is the information that teaches AI Agent what to say. Think of it as the AI Agent's study materials.

The Rules of Good Content

❌ Bad Example

The tumbler is made of stainless steel and keeps drinks hot for 6 hours and cold for 12 hours and fits most cup holders.

Problem: Too many facts in one sentence. Hard for AI Agent to find specific information.

✅ Good Example

## Product Specifications

- The tumbler is made of 18/8 stainless steel.
- The tumbler keeps drinks hot for up to 6 hours.
- The tumbler keeps drinks cold for up to 12 hours.
- The tumbler fits most standard cup holders.

Better: One atomic fact per bullet. Easy to find and retrieve.

Content Format

## Category Heading

- [Atomic fact about the topic]
- [Another atomic fact about the topic]
- [Another atomic fact about the topic]

🎯 Guidance — How AI Agent Behaves

+

Guidance tells the AI Agent how to behave, not what to know. It covers tone, rules, and escalation.

Guidance Format

## Category Heading

- IF [customer situation] THEN [what the AI Agent should do]

Examples

## Escalation Rules

- IF customer requests refund over $100 THEN hand over to human agent.
- IF handing over to human THEN say: "Let me connect you with a specialist."

## Tone Guidelines

- IF customer is frustrated THEN acknowledge their frustration first.
- IF customer is frustrated THEN use empathetic language.
- IF customer is frustrated THEN offer a clear solution.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Writing content as long paragraphs instead of Q&A format
  • Mixing facts (Content) and behavior rules (Guidance) in the same document
  • Using internal jargon that customers wouldn't use
  • Not keeping content atomic — cramming multiple facts into one answer
Tap to flip
Content vs Guidance
What's the difference?
Content = Facts the AI Agent knows (Q&A format)

Guidance = How AI Agent behaves (IF-THEN rules)
Quick Check
"Always apologize before explaining a policy" belongs in:
Content — it's information the AI Agent needs to know

Stage 2

🧪 Test: Verify Before Going Live

Run simulated customer conversations to see exactly how AI Agent will behave. Find and fix problems before real customers see them.

📝 Query

The customer's question. Use real customer language, not formal or technical words.

✅ Response

The ideal correct answer. Keep it atomic — one fact per line, clear and direct.

🎬 Action

What should happen: close (resolved), handover (to human), ignore, or snooze.

Understanding Test Results

Low Score What It Means How to Fix
Low Faithfulness
(below 0.9) 		AI Agent made up information OR content is missing facts Go to Train → Add correct content
Low Similarity
(below 0.7) 		AI Agent's answer has different meaning than expected Check if AI Agent is actually wrong, or update the benchmark reference answer

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Writing benchmark queries in formal language instead of how real customers talk
  • Setting reference answers that are too strict or too vague
  • Not testing edge cases: angry customers, typos, multiple questions in one message
  • Ignoring low scores instead of investigating root causes

📖 Glossary

AI Agent
An AI-powered virtual assistant that handles customer conversations automatically. It uses Content and Guidance to provide accurate, on-brand responses.
Atomic Content
One fact per statement. Don't combine multiple ideas into a single answer — keep each piece of information separate and clear.
Benchmark
A test case consisting of a Query (customer question), Response (expected answer), and Action (close, handover, ignore, or snooze).
Content
Facts and information the AI Agent knows. Written in atomic markdown format with ## headers and bullet points.
CSAT
Customer Satisfaction score. A direct measure of how happy customers are with AI Agent interactions. Higher is better.
Faithfulness
Score (0-1) measuring whether the AI Agent sticks to trained facts and doesn't make things up. Target: ≥0.9
Flywheel
The continuous improvement cycle: Train → Test → Deploy → Analyze → Repeat. The more you use it, the better your AI Agent gets.
Guidance
Rules for AI Agent behavior: tone, escalation triggers, and situational responses. Format: IF [situation] THEN [action].
Handover
When the AI Agent transfers a conversation to a human agent. High handover % defeats the purpose of AI Agent automation.
Resolution Rate
Percentage of customer queries the AI Agent resolves without human help. Higher = fewer human agents needed = lower cost.
Semantic Similarity
Score (0-1) measuring whether the AI Agent's meaning matches the expected reference answer. Target: ≥0.7
